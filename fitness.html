The Benefits Of

Exercising

Strong Body, Strong mind!>

I'll tell u my secret to not drink so much coffee: Exercise.Yes, that's true. When u exercise, u are activating your body and also your brain. This what happend to me, i can got to bed late and wake up early, if i exercise i won't feel tired or exhausted through the day.

Again (if u have read the past articles), start small and take one step at the time. Start with a 4 or 8 min exercise and then u can icrease your time slowly. Also, try to start with a specif part of your body that u want to strengthen. As time as passed and u increase your exercising time, u can add another part and so on.

U don't need to exercise for an hour to feel your body active. U just need the right exercise, discipline and to enjoy the process.As always, these are experience advices. Six months ago i start this fitness journey, and i started doing four minutes a day, and now i do sixteen minutes a day.

It's okay if u skip some days, or a month (i did it myself). If u have a fitness goal, sooner or later u will come back to the journey.U don't need to exercise everyday, give a break to yourself and to your body as well, is important to organize your schedule, so u have time to God, yourself, and your lover ones too.

Important tip: Never give up!Fex it Pink in one of their post from 2017, talked about exercising and mental health (...) You probably remember this quote from Elle Wood: Exercise gives u endorphins, endorphins makes u happy. Happy people don't kill their husbands!(...) Find what works best for u and ur body to make u feel happy. Checkout their article.

Besides endorphins, there are lots of ways that exercise can benefit your mental health, such as: The levels of chemicals in the brain, such as serotonin, stress hormones and endorphins, change when you exercise. Regular exercise can help you sleep better. And good sleep helps you manage your mood.

Exercise is a proven mood booster, a heart rate increase can reduce stress-induced brain damage, boost self-esteem, better sleep and brain boos are just few of the benefits that exercising can provide.

About 1.25 hours of brisk walking per week could yield an 18% lower risk of depression compared with not exercising, according to a new meta-analysis. It is 1.5 times more effective at reducing mild-to-moderate symptoms of depression, psychological stress, and anxiety than medication or cognitive behavior therapy, according to study's lead author, Dr. Ben Singh.
